The Hands Behind the Craft

Shaping stone
since 1998

For more than two decades, Ravichandran has dedicated his life to the art of stone carving. Beginning his journey in 1998, he has completed numerous sculpture and architectural projects across India — creating works that combine traditional techniques with lasting durability.

Alongside him stands Iyappan, a skilled artisan and collaborator. Together, they carry forward the responsibility of designing, carving, and finishing each work personally. Every project is shaped by their experience, precision, and commitment.

Every sculpture is handmade — no machine carving, only skilled hands and time.

Their creations serve temples, homes, public spaces, and architectural projects across India. Export work is undertaken when required.

The Granite
Pillar Tradition

One of Ravichandran's most recognized works is the single-stone granite pillar sculpture. Carved entirely from a single block of granite, the pillar rises in elegant form.

animal figures

Our Process

From stone
to sculpture

No shortcuts. No automation. Only the accumulated knowledge of decades, applied by hand.

Step 01

Stone Selection

Choosing the right granite or limestone block — evaluating grain, texture, and structural integrity for the intended form.

Step 02

Hand Carving

Rough shaping using traditional tools. The outer form is revealed before detailing begins. No machine assistance at any stage.

Step 03

Detailing

Precision carving and artistic refinement — where the sculpture gains character, expression, and its final form.

Step 04

Finishing

Polishing, texture work, and final inspection. Every sculpture is reviewed before delivery or installation.

MEMORY
Philosophy
Stone carries memory.

Unlike modern materials, stone endures generations. Each sculpture created in our workshop is meant not just for today, but for decades to come — preserving culture, devotion, and artistry in permanent form. When you commission a piece, you receive a continuation of tradition.
